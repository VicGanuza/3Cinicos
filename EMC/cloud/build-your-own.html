Running IT as a Service

Build Your Own

  • $14.5B will be spent on private cloud infrastructure in 2014, growing at 18% through 2017 (IDC)
  • 78% of all workloads will run on private cloud in 2016, with IaaS growing at 42% (IDC, Gartner, EMC)

Top 3 Challenges

  1. IT organizations are competing for customers with "Shadow IT"

    End users and LOBs are circumventing corporate IT in favor of public cloud offerings that are not available internally. This approach adds risk, cost, and complexity to the management of each application. Adherence to corporate standards for protection, security, and availability are compromised.

    Implementing an IaaS-based solution on EMC & VMware provides the solid foundation for IT to offer additional services with agility while maintaining corporate standards.

  2. Increasing efficiency to match public cloud providers

    While many organizations have achieved a high level of virtualization, they are not using their resources efficiently and are over-provisioning their infrastructure and virtual servers. They recognize that there is significant waste, but are unable to narrow the gap.

    EMC & VMware provide automated lifecycle management, real-time analytics, and historical reporting; designed to make the most out of the customers CAPEX and OPEX investments.

  3. IT needs to be able to demonstrate value to the business

    IT organizations are seen as a cost-center by the business and are looking for ways to better determine project and investment priorities as they manage their data centers to narrowing budget resources. They are searching for new methods to connect resource consumers with OPEX and CAPEX costs.

    EMCâ€™s Hybrid Cloud solution provides increased visibility into the true costs of IT services and can map resource consumption to the application and infrastructure owners.

Qualifying Questions

  • VP: Do your teams struggle to meet infrastructure demands, knowing that most workloads are over-provisioned in the data center, but app owners won't give up any of the resources?

    The EMC Hybrid Cloud solution has the ability to report on actual resource utilization at each layer of the infrastructure. This information can be used to intelligently alert cloud consumers and admins as to where resources are being wasted. Application workloads can then be "right-sized" based on real data as opposed to speculation. Implemented properly in the context of a chargeback model, everyone benefits from more efficient use of reclaimed physical resources.

  • LOB: Are your IT teams responsive enough to meet your demands for agility and service levels and do they offer the types of services that you need to run your business?

    Automation and Orchestration capabilities exposed through a self-service portal are the centerpiece of the EMC Hybrid Cloud solution. Through the use of a service catalog, cloud consumers are able to experience an "App-Store" like experience in selecting services to consume from the cloud. Cloud administrators are able to build sets of services based on business requirements that align with corporate policies for security, compliance, and availability.

  • IT: Do your IT Operations and Support teams have the tools and processes in place to be proactive, or are they constantly fighting fires with regards to availability and performance?

    The EMC Hybrid Cloud solution enables users that are consuming cloud resources to define the service levels that they need, and are willing to pay for to receive. Through transparent chargeback and showback models, the business is able to select the most appropriate services matched to their workloads while balancing the business value. Customers should also consider consulting and educational services to help jumpstart their transformation.

Objection Handling

  • "I have VMware already and I can deploy virtual machines from templates, that's my cloud. If I'm already highly virtualized, why do I need IaaS?"

    Having a highly virtualized data center is a great foundation, but IaaS adds elements of multi-tenancy, self-service, metering/chargeback, analytics, automation and orchestration; enabling the value and benefits of virtualization to extend to the business, beyond the walls of IT. Experiencing agility while maintaining governance, control, and compliance over application workloads enables a much faster time-to-value for new application resources and adds value directly to the business.

  • "Why do I need EMC when VMware already works with all of the other storage that I have? We have lots of vendors to choose from."

    EMC offers the best integration with VMware, as has been reported by Wikibon for several years running. That level of integration continues into our IaaS offerings as we develop additional support for VMware's automation and orchestration products.

    In addition to our storage arrays, EMC completes the IaaS story by also offering the best backup, replication, security, and management tools that align to meet the requirements of your business users.

  • "I like the idea of IaaS, and it sounds great. But this seems too complicated for our organization to consume."

    EMC can help you build a roadmap to "cloud" based on YOUR priorities. In most customers, IaaS is the easiest form of "cloud" to consume, and also aligns best with their current highly virtualized environments. Our Services teams and IaaS reference architectures can help to remove both risk and complexity.

    Not everything needs to be done at once; a gradual introduction of IaaS to your users will offer your organization some quick wins and offset the higher costs of eventually playing "catch up".
